How It Works

Simply fill out the subscription form below, filling out what type of milk product you would like to be delivered to your door. Be sure to include which days you would like have a delivery. You can opt for having your milk powdered, otherwise it will be delivered in glass bottles. Each item has a flat rate of $3.00 per quantity, per day.

You will be assigned a delivery date when you register based on your zip code. You will need to place a cooler outside your home (preferably in the shade) by 6 am. The cooler needs to be able to accommodate bottles that are 11 inches tall and big enough to hold your entire order. Dairy boxes are not supplied, as they can be expensive. If you would prefer a dairy box, there are several options that you can purchase online. The cooler MUST have ice or Icepaks in it. The delivery cannot be complete without them. A good option is to put two or three plastic water bottles in your freezer and reuse them in your cooler weekly.

Sourcing

Our Holsteins and Brown Swiss — possibly the oldest of all dairy breeds — graze outdoors from April until November, and during the winter they eat our farm-grown corn and hay. The cows also get silage and a few pounds of grain each day, making for a healthy diet. When they’re healthy, your drinks are too, because it’s reflected in the milk they produce. Just as important as what they eat is what our cows don’t eat. We don’t inject artificial growth hormones to boost milk production or plant crops with genetically modified seeds. We also fertilize with composted manure straight from the cows.

We process our milk as little as allowable to keep the important enzymes and “good” bacteria there to work for you. The fancy term for this old-fashioned method is low-temperature vat pasteurization. The milk is held at 145 degrees for 30 minutes and then cooled as quickly as possible. This gives milk (the kind many of us remember as kids) a smooth, sweet taste and preserves the naturally occurring enzymes that help your body to easily digest it. Granted, this process takes more effort but we don't mind. The great taste is worth every minute. Our milk also is unhomogenized, a rare find these days and a return to nostalgia when a thick layer of cream rose to the top of each bottle.

With our milk you’re getting all of the good, and none of the bad.
